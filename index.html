OpenSoC4HE: Open SoCs for Higher Education in Computer Architecture

A community hub for open-source hardware — and the teaching around it — in the classroom.

Abstract

Europe's path to digital sovereignty, anchored by the EU Chips Act and the European Processor Initiative (EPI), critically depends on training a new generation of engineers capable of designing and verifying custom silicon.

Yet computer architecture and digital design courses across European universities still largely rely on closed, vendor-locked toolchains and abstract software simulators, leaving graduates one step removed from real-world silicon design flows. The open-source hardware movement, and the RISC-V instruction set architecture in particular, has catalyzed a shift: open-source educational System-on-Chips are starting to close the gap between architectural theory and physical hardware implementation.

A critical element is still missing, though: a dedicated venue where the faculty who deploy these platforms in undergraduate and graduate classrooms can meet, compare deployment experiences, exchange teaching materials, and align on a common academic roadmap. OpenSoC4HE fills this gap within the HiPEAC community, with an explicit focus on the education layer upon which the entire open-silicon stack ultimately depends.

The workshop features "turn-key" platforms such as Simply-V, and works through the practical hurdles teams actually hit — from choosing cost-effective FPGA boards, to bringing verification into the undergraduate curriculum, to teaching a full RTL-to-GDSII tape-out with both open-source and commercial EDA flows. Its main deliverable is a community-driven, open-access roadmap that lowers the barrier for others to adopt open-source hardware in their teaching.

Topics of interest

We welcome contributions on, but not limited to:

  • Open-source educational SoCs and cores
  • Course design and curricula built on open hardware (BSc, MSc, PhD)
  • Hands-on labs, FPGA prototyping, and bring-your-own-device teaching setups
  • Teaching hardware verification (cocotb, UVM, formal methods) alongside RTL design
  • RTL-to-GDSII and tape-out experiences in the classroom (open PDKs, MPW shuttles)
  • Teaching with EDA toolchains — open-source and proprietary/commercial flows
  • Reusable teaching material, assessment rubrics, and open educational resources
  • Bridging digital logic, computer architecture, and SoC integration
  • Industry–academia alignment and the semiconductor skills pipeline
  • Experience reports: what worked, what failed, and lessons learned

Focus areas

Turn-key platforms

Ready-to-teach open SoCs such as Simply-V.

FPGA prototyping

Choosing cost-effective, bring-your-own-device boards for the classroom.

Hardware verification

Bringing cocotb, UVM, and formal methods into the undergraduate curriculum.

Tape-out in the classroom

Full RTL-to-GDSII flows, open PDKs, and MPW shuttles as teaching experiences.

EDA toolchains, open & proprietary

Comparing commercial and open-source EDA flows, and how to teach both.

Open roadmap & OER

Reusable materials, rubrics, and a community-driven adoption path.

Workshop format

OpenSoC4HE combines several formats across the day:

  • Invited talks from educators and practitioners in open silicon
  • Contributed papers and experience reports
  • A hands-on tutorial on an open educational SoC
  • Poster fast-pitches
  • A closing community panel
Main deliverable: a shared community roadmap document, drafted live during the closing panel and published with open access — the lasting output the workshop is built around.

Call for contributions

Contributors may choose one of three tracks:

Full paper

A complete contribution presenting methods, platforms, or results.

Short paper / experience report

A focused piece or a classroom experience report: what worked, what didn't.

Talk or demo only

Present without an accompanying paper — featured in the program.

Accepted papers follow a double-blind peer-review process and, if accepted, are published open-access in the HiPEAC workshop proceedings by Schloss Dagstuhl, indexed in DBLP and Scopus. Talk-only and demo-only contributions are featured in the program without an accompanying paper.

To express interest, send us the title of your intended contribution at your earliest convenience. The submission portal (EasyChair) link will be added here once it is live.
