OpenSoC4HE: Open SoCs for Higher Education in Computer Architecture

A community hub for open-source hardware in the classroom.

Abstract

Europe's path to digital sovereignty depends on training a new generation of engineers who can design and verify custom silicon. Yet, computer architecture courses often remain tethered to closed, vendor-locked toolchains that obscure the design flow.

OpenSoC4HE aims to bridge this gap by establishing a collaborative hub within the HiPEAC community for faculty to swap deployment experiences, share open-source teaching materials, and align on a roadmap for educational SoC adoption. The workshop will feature "turn-key" platforms—such as Simply-V—and explore practical hurdles: from selecting cost-effective FPGA prototyping boards to integrating hardware verification into the undergraduate curriculum.

The final output will be a community-driven, open-access roadmap designed to lower the barrier for others to adopt open-source hardware in their teaching.
